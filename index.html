About the ISBA Society

The International Society for Biomolecular Archaeology (ISBA) is an international community of researchers working on degraded biological remains from a range of archaeological and paleontological contexts. The society aims to promote the study of ancient biomolecules to better understand our shared human past.

News and Updates

Check out our website's News page or follow us on Twitter at[@ISBArchaeology](https://twitter.com/ISBArchaeology) to learn about our upcoming meetings, events, and other activities. We also support a [Career](/category/career) page with advertised studentships and job listings.

Society Membership

Membership in the society is open, and subscriptions are kept as low to allow as many people to join as possible. Among other benefits, members receive discounted registration at our bi-annual meetings, and membership subscriptions support our bi-annual research prizes and awards, as well as micro-grants for workshops. Membership is required for award and prize eligibility, as well as for applications for workshop micro-grants. For more information visit our [Membership](/membership) page.
